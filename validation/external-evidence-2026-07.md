# External evidence: controlled UX study of AI vs human websites

Source: Martinovikj, I. (2025). "An Insight into AI-Driven Website Design: The Impact on User Experience." Master's thesis, University of Ljubljana, School of Economics and Business (mentor doc. dr. Luka Tomat). Controlled comparative study: 48 participants, four live sites in two matched industry pairs (fashion: Evolve Clothing AI-generated via Wix vs Nour Hammour agency-built; travel: Islango AI vs Cain Travel agency-built), 5-point Likert across ten UX dimensions from WebQual 4.0 and the UX Honeycomb, best/worst votes, and VADER sentiment analysis of open-ended feedback.

This is a different evidence class from the project's 346-comment discourse study: controlled participants versus public sentiment. The two corroborate each other, which strengthens both.

## Findings leveraged into the skill (same day)

1. **The trust deficit.** The AI sites won overall (fashion +0.22, travel +0.08 mean difference) and beat human sites on Navigation, Layout, Engagement, and Return Intent, but LOST the same two dimensions in BOTH pairs: Trust and Security (-0.02, -0.19) and Credibility and Professionalism (-0.10, -0.15). This measured, replicated-across-pairs deficit motivated: the new Phase 1 axis 7 (Credibility) and the trust-surface gate (who is behind this, how to reach them, what are the terms) in checklist.md. The study also notes human sites carry real credits (agency, named designers); the skill's honesty rules already ban invented ones.

2. **The suspicion inversion.** Asked which site was AI-generated, participants most often suspected Cain Travel, the HUMAN agency-built site: "highly polished and generic layouts tend to create a perception of AI-generation," and per the cited literature (Lun et al., 2024) that perception decreases willingness to form emotional bonds with the brand. This is third-party validation of the entire grammar-tells program: genericness reads as AI regardless of true origin, and the perception carries a real UX cost. The thesis's prescription, "deliberately make human-centered choices that counterbalance AI's algorithmic precision," is what the grammar breaks, atmosphere layer, and human-genome rows implement.

3. **Literature anchors** now attachable to existing rules: Ghorbani (2024) and Upadhyaya (2024) on AI's "clean but bland and homogeneous visual outputs" via "over-automation" (matches the homogeneity cluster in the sentiment study); Jongmans et al. (2022) on the usability-to-pleasure pathway (aesthetics drive perceived usability, supporting the atmosphere layer).

## Weight and limitations

48 participants, two industries, one AI platform (Wix ADI, not code-model generation), cross-sectional. Treated as directional corroboration, not proof; its value is convergence with independently-gathered discourse evidence and the owner's own blind rankings, all three of which now point at the same two conclusions: AI design competes on polish and loses on trust and distinctiveness.
