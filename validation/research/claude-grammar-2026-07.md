# The Claude grammar: forensics, 2026-07-18

The owner reported that all runs of BOTH design skills still read as "typical Claude sites, all clean presentation" despite rotated fonts, palettes, and structures, and supplied five real Claude-built production sites as reference. Forensic corpus: those five sites (no shared brief: a personal brand, a consultancy, a musician, a creative collective, a community page) plus the seven Wayfare test runs. Each analyzed for compositional grammar, then synthesized. The five production sites are the control that kills the brief confound: they converge on the same grammar with no shared brief.

## The seven grammar tells

### 1. The hairline band metronome

The page is one centered max-width container (1020-1240px) holding a vertical stack of sections that all share a single global padding value (72px, 74px, 96px, or one clamp) and one 1px token hairline separator, usually stamped by a single global rule like `section { padding: 96px 0; border-bottom: 1px solid var(--line) }`. No section ever changes layout mode, container width, or vertical rhythm; full-bleed appears only as sanctioned 'punctuation bands' (marquee, stat strip, CTA flip). Density is identical whether the section holds a dense discography or one sentence.

Where: All 5 sites (hannsxpeter 72px, sharetact 74px, cityfidelia 96px with the border on the global section rule, ensemble clamp(3.5rem,...,7rem), newfrnds band-stack with --line on every band) and all 7 wayfare runs (96-128px, 'metronomically even' in every one).

Why it reads as Claude: It is layout-as-a-loop: the model writes one section template and iterates it N times, so vertical cadence is perfectly even regardless of content weight. Humans pace by content; this paces by template. It is the deepest structural driver of 'all clean presentation, all alike' because it survives any font or palette swap.

Counter encoded: Ban global section padding rules (grep test: no bare `section {` selector setting padding). Section vertical paddings on a page must take at least 3 distinct values with max/min >= 2.5x. At least one band must escape the master container: a full-bleed asset, an element with negative margin >= 64px, or a second container width differing by >= 25%. Yes/no test applied twice per page: 'do these two adjacent sections share both padding value and separator treatment?' must be no at least twice.

### 2. The eyebrow ceremony

Every h2 (often the h1 too) is preceded by the same micro-label unit: 0.55-0.85rem, uppercase, letter-spacing 0.06-0.42em, monospace or weight 600-700, frequently carrying an index number (01, 02...) and/or a flex-grow 1px hairline bar filling the rest of the row. The heading never arrives unannounced.

Where: All 12 pages: hannsxpeter (numbered mono eyebrow + flex bar), sharetact (identical device), cityfidelia (.eyebrow class above every h2), ensemble ('01 - Roster' numbered dash eyebrows on a 2px rule), newfrnds (.eyebrow/.lab above every section title), all 7 wayfare runs (uppercase micro-kicker, mono in the fd runs).

Why it reads as Claude: It is the single most repeated visible micro-signature in the corpus, and it converts every heading into an identical ceremonial unit, telegraphing 'templated chapter' rather than 'designed moment'. The user has already logged mono kickers and numbered chapters as personal Claude tells; this corpus confirms they survive every rotation.

Counter encoded: Hard cap: at most 1 element per page outside the nav matching (text-transform: uppercase) AND (letter-spacing >= 0.06em) AND (font-size <= 0.85rem). Numbered section indices (01/02/03) banned outright. Deck change: replace the kicker slot with a rotating heading-introduction deck (bare heading with nothing above it, run-in bold first line of the paragraph, oversized standalone numeral, marginal side-note, image lead-in) where 'nothing above it' must be drawn for at least half the sections.

### 3. Three-caste type with a barbell scale

A rigid role-pure font system: one display face used only for headings (single weight, often condensed uppercase or heavy 800 with negative tracking, line-height 0.78-1.1), one neutral body face at 16-17px / 1.55-1.65, and a monospace reserved for all chrome (nav links, buttons, labels, tags, footers, years). The scale is bimodal: clamp()-fluid headlines at 4:1 to 12:1 versus body, tiny wide-tracked labels below, and almost nothing living between roughly 1.5rem and 2.8rem. Weights are binary (400 vs 700-800); emphasis is done by recoloring spans, never by weight or true italics.

Where: All 5 sites (hannsxpeter mono/sans-800/serif; sharetact mono/Helvetica-800/Georgia; cityfidelia Space Mono/Anton/Inter; ensemble JetBrains Mono/Anton/Hanken with an explicit 'bimodal gap' note; newfrnds Anton/Inter 12:1 span) and all wayfare runs (identical three-slot architecture, mono third slot in 5 of 7).

Why it reads as Claude: Role purity is machine logic: each face has exactly one job and never trespasses. Human typography blurs castes (display face in a pull quote, bolded body as a subhead, working mid-scale subheads). Mono-for-chrome in particular is a Claude default, not an industry one.

Counter encoded: Mono may only set literal code, data, or tabular figures (yes/no grep: any nav link, button, eyebrow, or footer set in the mono stack = fail). Require >= 2 text styles in the 1.4-2.6rem mid register carrying real content (subheads, pull quotes, intro paragraphs). Require >= 3 distinct weights of the body face in running content. Cap clamp() at 4 font-size declarations per page; remaining sizes must be fixed steps.

### 4. One rationed accent and the accent-word headline

Roughly 88-92% neutral surface with exactly one accent hue confined to micro-doses: kicker text, the primary button, status dots, 3px border strips, plus/dash list markers, and one recolored span inside the big headline. The accent usually ships with a hand-derived darker twin for hover, and gets exactly one sanctioned large-surface moment (a marquee strip or pre-footer CTA band). No second hue holds a real job; neutrals are warm paper off-whites or their near-black inversion, never pure #fff/#000.

Where: hannsxpeter (--accent/--accent-deep, .hl span per headline), sharetact (red em-spans, 3px strips), cityfidelia (gold, one marquee inversion), ensemble (.accent-word, accent CTA band), wayfare 6 of 7 in the coral/vermilion/gold basin with accent-span h1 in 5 of 7; newfrnds is the degenerate zero-accent case built entirely from white alpha steps.

Why it reads as Claude: Color is used as annotation, never as composition; and nominally independent palettes keep collapsing into the same coral/gold attractor basin on warm paper, which matches the user's logged gold/cream/terracotta tells. The one-colored-word-in-the-headline is a copyable verbal-visual tic.

Counter encoded: Ban recolored spans inside h1/h2 (grep: no span/em with a color rule inside heading markup). Require either (a) >= 2 distinct hues each covering >= 10% of at least one viewport, or (b) one full section set as a duotone of the accent. Deck change: hue deck must force rotation through cool and desaturated bands, with coral/vermilion/amber/gold slots limited to 1 draw in 8; accent total surface coverage must be >= 12% of the page or exactly 0% (annotation-only rationing between 1% and 12% is the ba

### 5. Uniform finish with quarantined imperfection

Every page ships at one flat polish level: complete :root semantic token sheet (color roles, spacing ladder, durations, one easing curve), one universal hover recipe (translateY(-1 to -3px) plus border/shadow shift), one radius value plus 999px pills, ch-unit max-widths and text-wrap:balance on everything, and full day-one hygiene (prefers-reduced-motion, aria labels, focus-visible, rel=noopener). Even disorder is systematized: glow blobs clipped with pointer-events:none, chip rotations tokenized at -3 to 1.6deg, missing content rendered as designed hatched-gradient placeholders and dashed ghost cards. Nothing is over-designed relative to anything else; nothing is unfinished.

Where: All 12 pages. Explicitly named 'the deepest shared trait' across the wayfare runs ('seven different skins, one identical degree of finish') and 'itself the tell' at ensemble; hannsxpeter 'emitted in one pass at uniform polish'.

Why it reads as Claude: Single-pass generation produces a flat polish gradient with zero iteration debt. Human sites have a polish topography: a hero someone obsessed over, a plain legal page, a hack that shipped. Uniform excellence everywhere is the least human property in the corpus, and it is what the owner is naming with 'all clean presentation'.

Counter encoded: Keep the hygiene (it is quality) but break the uniformity mechanically: require >= 3 distinct hover grammars per page, and translateY lift may be at most one of them (grep: count distinct :hover transform/color recipes). Require >= 5 spacing or size values that are NOT on the token ladder (deliberate magic numbers). Require exactly one un-systematized element per page: a hand-placed absolute position, an off-center crop, or a one-off treatment that appears once and has no class reuse. Ban design

### 6. Content symmetrized to the template

Content is padded or trimmed until it fits the frame instead of the frame fitting the content: card grids with perfectly even counts (4/4, 5+5, 6 tiles), min-height-equalized list rows, every section receiving the full eyebrow+h2+padding ceremony even when it holds one sentence, demo artifacts with balanced fabricated data, and copy lengths visibly equalized across sections so nothing runs long or gets cut short.

Where: hannsxpeter (4/4/6/3/5 counts, 'section content lengths visibly balanced'), sharetact (5-and-5 list items, 4-and-4 work items, 40px min-height rows), cityfidelia (one-sentence shows section given full display ceremony), wayfare runs (fixed prose-plus-artifact beat per section, shared Lisbon/Porto/09:00-19:00 demo universe, one slogan verbatim across two independent runs).

Why it reads as Claude: The template precedes the content, so the content is generated to the template's shape. Human pages are shaped by what actually exists: 7 projects shown as 7, a thin section left thin, a long case study allowed to dominate.

Counter encoded: At least one grid per page must have an item count that does not divide evenly by its column count (an orphan cell, or one cell spanning 2 columns). At least one section must be delivered with no heading ceremony at all (a bare line, a single image, a stat with no label). Ban min-height equalization on list rows. The shortest content section must be under 25% of the word count of the longest (measurable). Deck change: demo data drawn from a seeded per-run fake-data pool so no two runs share citi

### 7. The stock ornament kit and the single-climax arc

A small recurring prop library redressed per palette: off-canvas radial glow blob, duplicated-span translateX(-50%) marquee ticker, giant-numeral stat band with tiny mono label, hairline row-list resume rows, 999px pill chips, striped/hatched placeholder tiles, opacity+translateY(18px) scroll reveals; all arranged into one fixed narrative arc: dark or ceremonial open (sticky blurred nav mirroring a dark hero), even verses, exactly one background flip (dark interstitial or solid-accent CTA band) placed as the pre-footer climax, then a footer that mirrors the nav.

Where: Glow blob: hannsxpeter, sharetact, wayfare heroes. Marquee: cityfidelia, ensemble, newfrnds. Stat band: cityfidelia, ensemble. Row-list resume: cityfidelia, ensemble. Dark bookends with one inversion: hannsxpeter, sharetact, ensemble (accent CTA), newfrnds (particle-hero bookend reprised as CTA), wayfare CTA bands. Scroll reveals: ensemble, all fd wayfare runs. Nav/footer mirroring: hannsxpeter, s

Why it reads as Claude: Six props and one plot, reused across supposedly independent designs; the viewer's pattern-matcher catches the props even when hue and font rotate. The arc (open dark, even middle, one flip, close dark) is a fixed story shape rather than a designed one.

Counter encoded: Maintain an explicit deny-list in the deck: {off-canvas glow blob, marquee ticker, stat band with mono label, pill chips at 999px radius, hatched/striped placeholder, full-accent CTA band, mirrored nav/footer}; at most 1 item from the list may appear per page, chosen by seed. If a full-width color-flip band exists, it must not be the last or second-to-last band before the footer. Nav and footer may share background color or type treatment, never both (yes/no check).

## Brief confound

The wayfare briefs mandated nav, hero, three feature sections, pricing, and footer, so the identical macro spine of the 7 runs (section order and count, presence of a pricing grid and hero CTAs) is partly prescribed and should be discounted. But the confound is small and precisely bounded, for two reasons. First, the 5 production sites are the control group: a personal brand, a consultancy, a musician, a creative collective, and a community page, with no shared brief, and they converge on the same grammar anyway (band metronome, eyebrow ceremony, three-caste type, rationed accent, uniform finish). Second, within the wayfare runs the brief cannot explain anything below the spine: the 1.05fr/.95fr hero ratio in 4 of 7, the 96-128px uniform padding, the kicker ritual, mono chrome in the fd runs, the coral/vermilion basin in 6 of 7 nominally independent palettes, warm-paper off-whites in all, the accent-span h1 in 5 of 7, the shared Lisbon/Porto demo-data universe, a slogan repeated verbatim across two independent runs, and the identical degree of finish. Rough attribution: the brief explains perhaps 15-25% of the sameness (the part visible only as section order), while the 'reads as Claude' signal lives almost entirely in the sub-brief compositional grammar, which would look the same under any brief. Practical implication: counter-rules should target within-section grammar, ornament, and finish uniformity, not the page arc, because the arc is often legitimately dictated by the genre or the brief.

## What human studio sites do differently

At the grammar level, human studio sites differ on almost every axis these pages share. Pacing is content-driven, not templated: section heights and paddings vary wildly, dense passages sit next to near-empty breathers, and a page will change layout mode mid-scroll (full-bleed editorial image, then a tight grid, then a single centered sentence, then a horizontal scroller), where the Claude corpus never changes mode at all. Asymmetry is structural rather than decorative: overlapping elements, columns of unequal height left unequal, text wrapping around images, things cropped by the viewport on purpose; Claude's only asymmetry is a clipped glow blob or a tokenized 2-degree rotation. Studios build each site around one signature gesture amplified to excess (a custom cursor, a single weird typeface pushed everywhere, one scroll interaction) and let everything else stay plain, producing an uneven polish topography; Claude distributes medium-high polish evenly with no peak. Art direction leads the system: photography or illustration sets the palette and the type reacts to the images, instead of images being grayscale-filtered into a token sheet. Typography uses the mid register and blurs roles: working subheads at 1.5-2.5rem, display faces in body-scale pull quotes, real italics, three or four weights of one family doing hierarchy; headings frequently arrive with nothing above them. Counts are dictated by reality (7 projects shown as 7, an orphan card left orphaned), sections sometimes have no heading at all, nav and footer rarely mirror, and ornament is derived from the brand concept and appears exactly once rather than being drawn from a reusable kit. Finally, human sites carry visible iteration residue: magic numbers, one-off hacks, a plain legal page next to an obsessed-over hero; the total absence of that residue, every element resolved to the same degree in one pass, is itself the strongest tell the corpus shares.

The cadence eyebrow, h2, lead, grid repeats four times without variation; the only interruptions are the dark pull-quote band after the hero (a deliberate beat change) and the centered contact finale. There are no dense stretches and no sparse ones; nothing runs long, nothing is cut short, and section content lengths are visibly balanced against each other. It reads like verses of the same song rather than a page with moods.

**tidiness**: Maximally composed; nothing is raw or unresolved. Every element sits inside the one container, every border is 1px, every radius is 4-5px (999px only for pills), every hover is the same micro-lift (translateY(-1px) or -2px plus border-color to accent plus soft shadow). Even the missing photographs are systematized: placeholder tiles built from a repeating-linear-gradient diagonal stripe with a corner index number and an inset 1px highlight ring, i.e., absence rendered as a design system feature. The single loose element, the hero glow blob, is clipped by overflow:hidden and pointer-events:none. Accessibility and hygiene are complete on day one: prefers-reduced-motion wipe, aria-labels, rel=noopener everywhere, data-URI SVG favicon, a try/catch around the one line of JS.
