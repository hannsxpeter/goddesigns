Capture

Photograph a receipt, forward an email, or drop in a PDF. Ledgerbird reads the client, the amount, the invoice number, and the due date, then files the record and watches for the payment to land.

Function
Invoice capture and payment tracking
Input
Phone photo, PDF upload, or a forward to your private capture address
Reads
Client, amount, currency, invoice number, issue and due dates
Follow-up
Flags invoices past due and drafts a polite reminder you approve before it sends

Anything Ledgerbird misreads takes one tap to correct, and it learns the client for next time.

Sort

Connect your bank and card accounts and every transaction lands in a Schedule C category: software subscriptions under office expenses, the lens rental under equipment, the client lunch at fifty percent. You confirm; it remembers.

Function
Expense sorting into tax categories
Input
Bank and card feeds, plus captured receipts matched to transactions
Categories
The Schedule C lines freelancers actually use, including home studio and mileage
Rules
Sort a vendor once and every future charge from them follows the same rule

Business and personal on one card? Split any transaction, or mark a whole account as mixed.

Estimate

Freelance income arrives in lumps, and the IRS still wants four even payments. Ledgerbird recalculates your Form 1040-ES estimate as each invoice is paid, so the number is current the day a payment is due, not a year-end surprise.

Function
Quarterly estimated tax calculation
Input
Your captured income, sorted deductions, filing status, and state
Output
A federal and state payment figure for each 1040-ES deadline
Deadlines
Reminders ahead of the April, June, September, and January due dates

Estimates are computed from safe-harbor rules; your tax preparer can export everything at year end.
