If your studio is anything like the ones we come from, it runs on three systems: a spreadsheet for classes, a whiteboard grid for the kilns, and a drawer of punch cards for the money. All three work, right up until the Tuesday they don't.

Kilnhouse replaces those three systems with one quiet piece of software. Here is exactly what it does.

Class scheduling

Publish your term once. Members book and pay online, waitlists backfill cancellations on their own, and every instructor sees their roster before they walk in. When a wheel breaks and you cancel a session, everyone booked gets the message and a class credit without you touching a phone.

The firing queue

The whiteboard grid, made honest. Members tag their pieces for bisque or glaze, you load by cone and shelf space, and the queue updates the moment a kiln door closes. When the glaze fire comes out, everyone with work inside gets a note. No more "is my mug out yet" at the front desk.

Member billing

Memberships, class fees, clay, and firing charges land on one monthly invoice, and autopay collects it. A member's page shows what they owe, what they fired, and when their plan renews. The drawer of punch cards is retired with full honors.
