Wheel at six, handbuilding at eight, waitlists that work themselves.

Build recurring sessions once and let Kilnhouse do the arithmetic: seats per wheel, instructor assignments, make-up credits, and a waitlist that promotes people the moment a seat opens.

  • Recurring series, workshops, and one-off date nights
  • Capacity tracked per wheel, per table, per kiln shelf
  • Self-serve booking and cancellation windows you control

02 / Firing queue

A queue your kiln can actually keep.

Members log work onto the shelf from their phone. Staff plan loads by shelf space and cone, and everyone watches their pieces move from shelf to loaded to firing to cooling to ready, with a text at the end.

  • Bisque and glaze queues per kiln, sorted by cone
  • Load planning by shelf, half-shelf, and post height
  • Firing logs with schedules, witness cones, and notes
  • Automatic "your work is ready" notifications

03 / Member billing

Memberships that charge themselves.

Monthly memberships, clay by the bag, firing fees by the cubic inch: Kilnhouse meters it as it happens and settles it on one invoice. Failed cards retry themselves, and nobody chases a Venmo again.

  • Recurring tiers, punch cards, and drop-in passes
  • Firing fees metered by volume or by shelf
  • Clay, tools, and glaze sales on the member tab
  • Dunning, retries, and proration handled quietly
