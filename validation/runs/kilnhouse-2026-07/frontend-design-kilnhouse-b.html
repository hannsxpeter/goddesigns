Kiln firing queue

A firing queue the whole studio can see.

Members claim shelf space from their phone. You schedule the firing, log the program, and Kilnhouse texts everyone when their ware is cool enough to touch. The kiln log writes itself, cone by cone.

  • Shelf claims with photosEach piece tagged to its owner. Unloads stop being archaeology.
  • Programs and cone history per kilnElement hours tracked, so you replace them before a ruined glaze load.
  • "Your ware is ready" textsSent when the kiln crosses 150°F on the way down. Not before.

Member billing

Dues, clay, and firing fees. Settled without a spreadsheet.

Memberships renew on autopay. Clay sells by the pound at the counter. Firing fees post when the kiln closes, priced by the half shelf or by volume, however your studio does it. One statement, one charge, zero chasing.

  • Failed cards handled politelySmart retries, then a friendly nudge. You never send that email again.
  • Firing fees post from the queueClose the kiln log and every owner's share lands on their statement.
