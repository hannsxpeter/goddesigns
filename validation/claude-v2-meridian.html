How a trip comes together.

The long conversation

Planning starts with an unhurried call about pace, not places. How you like mornings. Whether you read on trains. What ruined your last holiday. Destinations come later; they are the easy part.

The field-checked draft

Your planner drafts a route built only from places we have slept, eaten, and gotten lost in ourselves. You push back, we redraw. The draft is done when nothing in it feels like a checklist.

On the road, on call

While you travel, the planner who drew your route stays reachable. A ferry cancelled, a village festival worth rerouting for: you message, we replan, the trip keeps its pace.

A small studio, on purpose.

Meridian takes on a handful of trips each season, because a route drawn carefully takes weeks and we will not draw one any other way. Every planner travels the regions they plan, on the trains and ferries and footpaths they recommend, in the seasons they recommend them.

We take no commissions from hotels, guides, or operators. Planning fees are our only income, which means every recommendation in your route is there because we would choose it ourselves.
