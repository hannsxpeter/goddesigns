Eight wheels, twelve seats, one calendar

Set a class once and Kilnhouse runs it: repeating sessions, capacity that matches your floor, and a waitlist that manages itself.

  • Recurring sessions Build Tuesday wheel throwing once. It repeats every week until you end it, holidays excluded.
  • Capacity from your floor Seats equal wheels. When someone drops, the waitlist promotes the next member and tells them.
  • Member self-serve Members book, reschedule, and cancel on their own, inside the window you allow.

One queue for every kiln

Members submit pieces, staff build loads by shelf space, and every kiln keeps its own program library. When a firing ends, the people with work inside hear about it first.

One invoice: membership, shelf, firings

Everything a member owes lands on a single monthly invoice, priced by rules you set once.

  • Membership autopay Monthly billing with proration for mid-month starts and pauses for injuries or travel.
  • Shelf rental A line item on the same invoice, not a second charge on a different day.
  • Firing fees Priced by the half shelf or by weight, logged straight from the firing queue.
