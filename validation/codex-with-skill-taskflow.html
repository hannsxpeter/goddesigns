Chapter 01 / The approach

Less status chasing. More shared context.

TaskFlow connects the brief, the work, and the decisions around it, so the next step is never buried in another thread.

  1. 01

    Begin with a useful brief

    Give every project an outcome, owner, timeline, and definition of done before tasks start multiplying.

  2. 02

    Make movement visible

    See what changed, what is blocked, and where a handoff needs attention without calling another status meeting.

  3. 03

    Keep the decision with the work

    Reviews, approvals, and updates stay attached to the project, giving the whole team a reliable record.

TaskFlow is built around a simple rule: people should be able to understand a project before asking its owner for an update.

Chapter 04 / Questions

Before you bring the work over.

A few straightforward answers about fitting TaskFlow into an existing team.

Is TaskFlow only for product teams?

No. Any team coordinating projects with owners, milestones, handoffs, and approvals can shape TaskFlow around its process.

Can we start with one team?

Yes. Begin with a focused workflow, learn what your team needs, and extend the same structure to connected teams when it is useful.

Will TaskFlow replace our communication tools?

It does not need to. TaskFlow gives project work a reliable home while chat and email remain useful for conversation.

How do we bring existing work in?

Start with active projects, their owners, and their next milestones. Add older context only when it helps a current decision.
