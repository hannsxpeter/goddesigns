The invoice files itself.

Invoice capture. Forward an invoice to your Ledgerbird address, or photograph a paper one, and it is read, recorded, and matched to the client. When the deposit lands in your connected account, the invoice marks itself paid.

  • Accepted formatsPDF, image, email body
  • Client matchingBy name, email, or bank memo
  • Payment statusReconciled against your bank feed
  • Late invoicesFlagged after your stated terms

Every expense knows its place.

Expense sorting. Connect the account and card you actually use. Ledgerbird sorts each charge into categories that fit creative work, and learns yours: the difference between a client lunch and a personal one, a reference book and a novel. You confirm or correct in one tap; it remembers.

  • Built forSoftware, gear, studio rent, materials, travel
  • Deduction notesAttached to every sorted charge
  • ReceiptsPhotograph once, stored with the charge
  • Mixed-use itemsSplit by percentage, kept consistent

April arrives. You already knew.

Quarterly tax estimates. From your recorded income and sorted deductions, Ledgerbird estimates each quarterly payment, tells you the due date, and shows the running amount to set aside this week. Come filing season, you hand your accountant one clean export instead of a shoebox.

  • Estimates forUS federal and state estimated tax
  • Due datesAll four deadlines, with reminders
  • Set-aside guidanceUpdated as each payment clears
  • Year-end exportSchedule C categories, CSV or PDF
Why the bird

Ledger keeping is old work: patient, exact, done in good light. We named the software after the magpie, a bird that collects small shining things and keeps them safe. That is the whole job.
