# Genome sourcing vantages (maintainer-only)

This file is NOT read during a design run. It is a contributor aid for finding real
human-built sites to extract deck rows 16+ from (`CONTRIBUTING.md`, "Contributing a
human-genome row"). The point of the human-genome lane is that a model cannot author
its way out of its own distribution, so genome rows must come from outside it. Left to
instinct, a maintainer keeps sampling the same admired corner of the web, so the sourced
rows re-converge. These vantages force the search to spread.

Stand at a vantage, then go find real sites built for or by people at that standpoint
(their trade associations, tools, storefronts, regulators, community boards). Extract
the genome from what real practitioners actually shipped, not from design galleries.

0. The regulated trade: a licensed profession's own sites (clinics, law, accountancy, inspection). Business-fact grammar, dense disclosure, trust surface as default.
1. The logistics floor: freight, warehousing, dispatch, fleet. Tables, codes, status, timetables carried as the primary content.
2. The on-call service: emergency and 24-hour trades (locksmith, plumber, tow, IT). Phone-forward, one action, no ceremony.
3. The school or club: community orgs, unions, hobby leagues, congregations. Notice-board density, real photos, unpolished warmth.
4. The market stall: independent commerce (butcher, hardware, chandlery, feed store). Product-fact tiles, price-forward, no lifestyle veneer.
5. The trade counter: B2B suppliers whose buyers are tradespeople, not consumers. Part numbers, spec rows, account-first flows (deck row 16 came from here).
6. The instrument maker: sites for people who read gauges and logs (lab supply, marine electronics, HVAC, brewing). Readouts and spec blocks as decoration.
7. The archive keeper: institutions organized around records (libraries, registries, museums, standards bodies). Provenance, catalog numbers, finding-aid structure.
8. The field operator: outdoor and mobile work (surveying, agriculture, fisheries, utilities). Weather, coordinates, and site conditions beside the work.
9. The night economy: venues, kitchens, transit, security that run after dark. Schedule and settlement grammar, drama plus data.

Intake rule (enforced in CONTRIBUTING.md): every genome row carries a provenance line
`Genome: vantage=<0-9> | source=<domain> | captured=<YYYY-MM>`, at most two rows share a
vantage, and the two newest genome rows must not share one, so the lane keeps spreading.
